# Building Systems That Scale

Building scalable systems isn't just about handling more usersâ€”it's about creating foundations that can evolve with changing business needs. Through projects like Argus and Zeno, I've developed principles that help systems scale both technically and operationally.

## Core Principles

**Modularity First**: Every component should be replaceable and testable in isolation. This isn't just good engineering; it's good business sense.

**Observable Everything**: If you can't measure it, you can't improve it. Build logging, metrics, and monitoring into every system from day one.

**Fail Fast, Recover Faster**: Design for failure scenarios from the beginning, not as an afterthought.

## Real-World Application

When building research automation tools, these principles meant the difference between a script that works once and a system that reliably processes hundreds of research requests daily.

The investment in proper architecture pays dividends when business requirements inevitably change.
