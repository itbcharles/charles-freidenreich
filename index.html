What I'm Drawn To

I'm drawn to the puzzle of efficiency. I think we should be able to use good information to make better decisions – in businesses, NGOs, and governments. That intersection of data, operations, and multi-variable decision-making is what I tend to be interested in.

Eagle Scout

I consider my life as an Eagle Scout to be among the most important. Becoming an Eagle Scout took a decade of my life. Long trips—canoeing in Minesota , trekking in Northern Sweden, it taught me – a spoiled kid – that I couldn't simply complain to make my way out of discomfort. I learned to work really hard, for a long period of time. I consider Scouting to be my source of grit, determination, and willpower.

Outdoors

I spent a lot of time outdoors as a Scout – I still do – but as I get older I realize what it means to me. I deeply care about the outdors, and being able to spend time in remote areas of it helps make me who I am. There's nothing quite like almost dying on a cliff side with your closest friends, or getting sniffed by a black bear in the middle of the night.

Argus

My main side project is Argus. I'm trying to build a tool that automates database compilation. The goal is to help people compile massive databases of niche information by searching the web with some elegant new techniques. The name Argus comes from Greek Mythos – a man who sees everything.

Now
