Eagle Scout

I consider being an Eagle Scout to be a big part of who I am. Becoming an Eagle Scout took a decade of my life. Long trips—canoeing in Minnesota, trekking in Northern Sweden, it taught me – a spoiled kid – that I couldn't simply complain to make my way out of discomfort. I learned to work really hard, for a long period of time. I consider Scouting to be my source of grit, determination, and willpower.

Outdoors

I spent a lot of time outdoors as a Scout – I still do – but as I get older I realize what it means to me. I deeply care about the outdors, and being able to spend time in remote areas of it helps make me who I am. There's nothing quite like almost dying on a cliff side with your closest friends, or getting sniffed by a black bear in the middle of the night.
